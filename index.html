About Maor, My Israeli Little Brother,

Theres so much positive to say about Maor, its challenging to know where to start and apprehensive to try to encompass all aspects in a reference. When I first read his profile, I was incredulous at how a young man who just started traveling could be so attuned to seeking meaningfulness in life over what most new, young globetrotters seek (ie checking touristy items off a bucket list or free place to stay).

He encompasses the true Couchsurfing spirit in that his primary goal while traveling is learning *about* and *from* his hosts, and experiencing the way his hosts live and appreciate parts of their hometown through their lens. He wants to, needs to ensure that he contributes during his stay and does whatever he can to help to take any onerous tasks off your busy schedule so that not only is he not a burden of a guest, but also brings into your life - relief, ease, calm.

He has also helped me immensely, simply by his natural inquisitiveness and deep analyses that brought about thought-provoking questions that helped me reflect on aspects of my life as well as process through my traumas and think of things in different ways...hes a natural therapist, better than most Ive ever worked with. All thisblows me away at how someone so youthful could be so wise beyond his years.

His drive for learning keeps him inspired w life everyday. Ive learned lots from Maor, including about myself. We also went over Krav Maga, and we worked through real-life scenarios that Id survived through, and he greatly valued being presented w thinking through the factors that a small female in a compromised position would be challenged by, that he & his class had not taken into consideration. He is excited to bring back the scenarios to address in his classes and implement more realistic approaches dependent on less advantageous body frame / strength / differing points of leverage. That means a lot to me knowing that he is leading a movement to help women empower themselves, which in turn leads to a greater, systemic change.

Hes a loving puppy parent by the way. My pup has grown lovingly attached to Maor and he to her. So many adorable, playful moments of the two cuddling and her being his lap dog (shes an XL dog breed).

I look forward to the next time we catch up, whether here, Hawaii, Israel, or elsewhere in the world. Hes welcome to pop up on my door step anytime!
